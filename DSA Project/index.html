Algorithm Explanation

Binary Search (Divide & Conquer)

Binary search works by repeatedly dividing the search space in half. It compares the target word with the middle element of the dictionary:

  • If the target word matches the middle element, the search is complete.
  • If the target word is alphabetically before the middle element, the search continues in the lower half.
  • If the target word is alphabetically after the middle element, the search continues in the upper half.

Time Complexity: O(log n) where n is the number of words in the dictionary.

Greedy Search

In this implementation, the greedy approach starts at the beginning of the dictionary and makes locally optimal choices at each step:

  • It compares the current word with the target word.
  • Based on the comparison, it decides how many positions to move forward (using the first letter comparison as a heuristic).
  • It continues this process until it finds the word or determines it's not in the dictionary.

Time Complexity: Can vary, but typically O(n) in the worst case, where n is the number of words in the dictionary.
